<YouPanel persona="jarod">
The forks tell you what kind of fed IT job you'd be applying for.

- **Federal direct** roles are the broad category: most fed sysadmin postings, civilian or DoD direct.
- **Defense contractor** roles (CMMC / DFARS-covered environments at private contractor companies) are a real and large slice of the job market, but this guide doesn't cover the fork in depth. If you target this path, the official CMMC / NIST 800-171 references and practitioner-written content are where to learn.
- **Classified** roles require a clearance. The employer usually sponsors it, but the timeline to get cleared is months to years. Different career path.
- **Cloud / FedRAMP** roles concentrate at SaaS companies selling to federal agencies. Often hybrid private/fed culture.
- **Industry standards** isn't fed at all but is the alternative path if you'd rather do security work outside the federal contract universe.

**First study move:** look at five job postings across these fork categories and notice the vocabulary differences. The same NIST baseline shows up everywhere; the additional vocabulary (CMMC, FedRAMP, ICD 503, SOC 2) tells you which fork the role is on.
</YouPanel>
