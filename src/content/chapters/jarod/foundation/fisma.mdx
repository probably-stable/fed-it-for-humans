FISMA is where the federal-IT vocabulary starts. Almost every job posting in this world mentions it — usually as "FISMA experience preferred" or "familiarity with FISMA / NIST RMF required." FISMA is where your future world starts. Every job posting in federal IT mentions it somewhere — usually as "FISMA experience preferred" or "familiarity with FISMA / NIST RMF required."

You don't need to memorize the law. You need to understand: it sits at the top, everything else flows from it, and "FISMA work" in practice means working with the things downstream of it (controls, assessments, authorizations, reports).

**First study move:** read FISMA's Wikipedia page, *not* the law text. The Wikipedia article is shorter, clearer, and gives you the historical context (the 2002 → 2014 modernization, what changed, why). The law text itself is a slog and you don't need it.
