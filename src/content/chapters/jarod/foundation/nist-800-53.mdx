"NIST 800-53 experience" appears in nearly every fed-IT job posting. The catalog itself is hundreds of controls across 20 families, way too much to memorize, and no interviewer expects you to. What they DO expect: recognize the format, name a few major families and what they cover, understand control baselines (Low / Moderate / High via 800-53B), and ideally know that controls can be system-specific, common, or hybrid (inheritance). That's interview-ready at most levels.

"NIST 800-53 experience" appears in nearly every fed IT job posting. You don't need to memorize the catalog, you need to recognize the format and the major families.

Interview-ready: know that 800-53 is the controls catalog (rev 5 current), organized into 20 families, with baselines Low / Moderate / High in companion document SP 800-53B. Be able to name 5-6 family codes and what they cover (AC = access control, AU = audit, CM = configuration management, IA = identification and authentication, SI = system and information integrity is a solid starter set). Bonus: mention control inheritance, knowing that controls can be system-specific, common, or hybrid signals you understand how the catalog actually gets used in practice.

**First study move:** read three controls end-to-end, AC-2 (Account Management), AU-2 (Event Logging), and CM-6 (Configuration Settings). They're representative of how controls read and they're three you'll see referenced constantly. NIST publishes 800-53 for free as a PDF on csrc.nist.gov.
