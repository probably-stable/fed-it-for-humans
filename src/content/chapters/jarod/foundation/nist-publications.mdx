NIST publications are the things you'll see referenced in every fed IT job posting. "NIST 800-53 experience" or "RMF familiarity" or "FIPS 199 categorization" — all NIST. You don't need to read the publications. You need to know which one covers which topic so the job descriptions stop reading as gibberish.

The shortest useful summary: **FIPS** = mandatory federal standards (199 = categorization, 200 = minimum requirements). **SP 800** = guidelines (37 = RMF lifecycle, 53 = controls catalog, 53A = assessment, 171 = CUI for contractors).

**First study move:** spend 30 minutes on the NIST CSRC website (csrc.nist.gov). Don't try to read any publication. Just browse the publication list and read the one-line abstract for each of the publications above. That gives you the map without the territory.
</YouPanel>
