"STIG experience" or "STIG hardening" appears in basically every fed sysadmin job posting. CIS shows up too, especially for cloud roles. You don't need to memorize the contents, you need to know what they are and which one each persona uses.

Quick interview-ready: STIGs are DISA's product hardening checklists, mandatory for DoD; CIS Benchmarks are CIS's voluntary hardening references, widely adopted in private sector and federal civilian. Both translate NIST controls into specific product settings. Many agencies maintain their own product-level implementation guides (gold images, agency-specific configurations) that may or may not derive from STIGs. Vendor guides (Microsoft, Red Hat, VMware, etc.) supplement when STIG/CIS coverage lags.

**First study move:** download one STIG and one CIS Benchmark for the same product (RHEL 9 is a good choice, both are publicly available) and compare a few findings. Seeing the same setting addressed by both formats teaches you the difference faster than reading about either.
