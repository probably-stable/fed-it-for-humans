RMF familiarity is the single most-cited NIST topic in fed-IT job postings. You don't need to know the seven steps in production-engineer-level depth, but you should be able to talk about them at a level that demonstrates real awareness: name them in order, explain roughly what each covers, recognize the key artifacts (SSP, POA&M, ATO) and the key roles (System Owner, ISSO, AO, 3PAO). That's enough for most sysadmin / engineer interviews.

<YouPanel persona="jarod">
"RMF familiarity" appears in almost every federal IT job posting. You don't need to memorize the seven steps, but you should know the lifecycle exists and what each step is roughly about.

Quick interview prep: if someone asks "what's the RMF," you should be able to say something like *"it's a NIST framework for authorizing and monitoring federal information systems, seven steps, starting with Categorize and ending with Monitor, with Prepare added at the front."* That's enough to demonstrate awareness.

**First study move:** read NIST SP 800-37's executive summary and Chapter 1. Skip the rest. The exec summary gives you the seven steps and the why; Chapter 1 gives you the vocabulary (System Owner, AO, ISSO, etc.). That's the fastest way to be conversant.
</YouPanel>
