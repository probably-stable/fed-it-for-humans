The acronyms compound fast in this world. This is the lookup table.

Each entry: the acronym or term, what it stands for if it's an acronym, a one-line plain-English definition, and (where useful) a pointer to the chapter that covers it in depth. Alphabetical. Browser search (Ctrl+F / Cmd+F) is your friend.

---

## A

**A-130** — OMB Circular A-130, "Managing Information as a Strategic Resource." OMB's policy directive that operationalizes FISMA into specific federal agency obligations. *See: [OMB Circular A-130](/sam/foundation/omb-a130).*

**AICPA** — American Institute of Certified Public Accountants. Authors and maintains SOC 2 (the attestation framework most enterprise customers ask SaaS vendors for).

**AI RMF** — NIST AI Risk Management Framework. Voluntary framework for managing AI-specific risks, released January 2023. Companion: NIST AI 600-1 (Generative AI profile, July 2024).

**AO** — Authorizing Official. The senior agency official who weighs the risk and either grants ATO or denies it at the Authorize step of the RMF. *See: [NIST RMF](/sam/foundation/nist-rmf).*

**ATO** — Authorization to Operate. The formal authorization granted by the AO that a federal information system can run. The major milestone of the RMF.

---

## C

**CAB (CMMC AB)** — CMMC Accreditation Body. The body that accredits CMMC assessors and oversees the CMMC program. (CMMC itself is out of scope for this guide.)

**CCPA / CPRA** — California Consumer Privacy Act / California Privacy Rights Act. State-level privacy laws regulating how organizations handle California residents' personal data.

**CIO** — Chief Information Officer. The senior agency or company executive responsible for IT. In federal agencies, owns the FISMA program.

**CIPP/E, CIPP/US** — Certified Information Privacy Professional (Europe / United States). Privacy-law certifications from the IAPP.

**CIS** — Center for Internet Security. Non-profit organization that publishes both the CIS Controls (program-level practices) and CIS Benchmarks (per-product hardening). *See: [Industry standards](/sam/forks/industry-standards) and [STIGs and CIS](/sam/foundation/stigs-and-cis).*

**CISA** — Two distinct things: (1) Certified Information Systems Auditor (audit certification from ISACA), and (2) Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency (federal agency under DHS). Context tells you which.

**CISO** — Chief Information Security Officer. The senior executive responsible for security. May report to the CIO or to the CEO depending on the org.

**CISSP** — Certified Information Systems Security Professional. Broad-scope security certification from (ISC)².

**CJIS** — Criminal Justice Information Services. FBI-administered program covering security requirements for criminal justice systems. Affects state/local law enforcement IT.

**CMMC** — Cybersecurity Maturity Model Certification. DoD certification program for defense contractors handling CUI in their own environments. **Out of scope for this guide.**

**CNSS** — Committee on National Security Systems. Federal interagency body that publishes guidance for national security systems.

**CNSSI 1253** — CNSS Instruction 1253. Categorization standard for national security systems (different impact dimensions than FIPS 199). Used in classified environments.

**CPA** — Colorado Privacy Act. State-level privacy law.

**CSF** — NIST Cybersecurity Framework. Voluntary federal-authored framework. CSF 2.0 (released 2024) has six functions: Govern, Identify, Protect, Detect, Respond, Recover. The most common bridge between federal and non-federal security thinking. *See: [Industry standards](/sam/forks/industry-standards).*

**CSP** — Cloud Service Provider. The company offering a cloud service. In FedRAMP context, the entity pursuing authorization.

**CSRC** — NIST Computer Security Resource Center. The arm of NIST that owns the security publications (FIPS, SP 800 series). Website: csrc.nist.gov.

**CUI** — Controlled Unclassified Information. Federal information that isn't classified but requires protection. Defined by Executive Order 13556 and the National Archives' CUI Registry.

---

## D

**DFARS** — Defense Federal Acquisition Regulation Supplement. The DoD-specific extension to the FAR (Federal Acquisition Regulation). DFARS clause 252.204-7012 is the one that pulls defense contractors into NIST 800-171 / CMMC obligations.

**DISA** — Defense Information Systems Agency. The DoD agency that publishes STIGs and SRGs. Website: public.cyber.mil.

**DoD** — Department of Defense.

**DoDI** — DoD Instruction. The document type DoD uses for binding department-wide policy. DoDI 8500.01 (cybersecurity) and DoDI 8510.01 (RMF for DoD IT) are the load-bearing ones for DoD IT compliance.

**DOE** — Department of Energy.

**DSC** — Desired State Configuration. Microsoft's PowerShell-based configuration management technology, often used to apply Windows hardening baselines.

---

## F

**FAR** — Federal Acquisition Regulation. The base federal procurement regulation. DFARS extends it for DoD.

**FedRAMP** — Federal Risk and Authorization Management Program. Standardized authorization program for cloud services used by federal agencies. *See: [FedRAMP](/sam/forks/fedramp).*

**FIPS** — Federal Information Processing Standards. Mandatory federal standards published by NIST. FIPS 199 (categorization), FIPS 200 (minimum requirements), FIPS 140-3 (cryptographic modules) are the load-bearing ones for security.

**FISMA** — Federal Information Security Modernization Act (originally Federal Information Security *Management* Act, 2002; modernized 2014). The federal law requiring every federal agency to have an information security program. *See: [FISMA](/sam/foundation/fisma).*

---

## G

**GDPR** — General Data Protection Regulation. EU privacy law. Applies to any organization processing EU residents' personal data, regardless of where the org is located.

**GPO** — Group Policy Object. Microsoft Windows configuration mechanism, often used to apply Windows hardening baselines (including STIG and CIS Benchmark settings).

**GSA** — General Services Administration. Federal agency that runs the FedRAMP PMO.

---

## H

**HCISPP** — HealthCare Information Security and Privacy Practitioner. Healthcare-focused security certification from (ISC)².

**HHS** — Department of Health and Human Services. Administers HIPAA via the Office for Civil Rights (OCR).

**HIPAA** — Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act. U.S. federal law governing healthcare privacy and security. The HIPAA Security Rule and Privacy Rule are the operational pieces. *See: [Industry standards](/sam/forks/industry-standards).*

**HITECH** — Health Information Technology for Economic and Clinical Health Act. 2009 law that strengthened HIPAA enforcement and breach notification.

**HITRUST** — Health Information Trust Alliance. Private framework (HITRUST CSF) bundling HIPAA + NIST + ISO + others into a healthcare-tailored certification.

---

## I

**IATT** — Interim Authorization to Test. A short-term ATO variant for systems still in test or development.

**IC** — Intelligence Community. The federation of U.S. intelligence agencies. Has its own IT compliance overlays (CNSSI 1253, ICD 503, JSIG).

**ICD** — Intelligence Community Directive. The IC's binding-policy document type. ICD 503 covers IT system security risk management for the IC.

**ICS** — Industrial Control Systems. The category of systems addressed by NIST SP 800-82 (now broader OT coverage).

**IG1, IG2, IG3** — CIS Controls Implementation Group 1 / 2 / 3. CIS's maturity-tiered adoption model. IG1 is the basic baseline; IG3 is full enterprise security.

**ISMS** — Information Security Management System. The organizational system ISO 27001 specifies as the unit of certification.

**ISO 27001 / 27002** — International standards for information security. ISO 27001 specifies the management system; ISO 27002 is the catalog of controls.

**ISSO** — Information System Security Officer. The day-to-day operational owner of system security at the agency level. Reports to the AO; works with system owners.

---

## J

**JAB** — Joint Authorization Board. The historical FedRAMP governance body composed of DHS, DoD, and GSA CIOs. The "JAB path" P-ATO is being restructured under FedRAMP modernization.

**JSIG** — Joint SAP Implementation Guide. The cybersecurity guide for Special Access Programs. Cleared environments only.

---

## L

**Li-SaaS** — Low-Impact SaaS. A FedRAMP impact level introduced in 2017 for collaboration / productivity SaaS that doesn't store significant federal data. Lighter than full Low.

---

## N

**NIST** — National Institute of Standards and Technology. U.S. federal agency under the Department of Commerce. Authors the FIPS standards and SP 800 series that drive federal information security. *See: [NIST publications](/sam/foundation/nist-publications).*

---

## O

**OCR** — Office for Civil Rights. The HHS office that enforces HIPAA.

**OMB** — Office of Management and Budget. White House office that issues Circulars (including A-130). Owns federal-wide IT policy.

**OT** — Operational Technology. Computers controlling physical things (industrial control systems, lab equipment, medical devices, etc.). Addressed by NIST SP 800-82.

---

## P

**P-ATO** — Provisional Authorization to Operate. The FedRAMP authorization issued by the JAB (rather than by an agency AO).

**PCI-DSS** — Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard. The mandatory framework for organizations handling payment card data. Currently version 4.0.

**PHI** — Protected Health Information. The category of health data HIPAA protects.

**PII** — Personally Identifiable Information. Information that can identify a specific individual. Protected under various laws (HIPAA, GDPR, state privacy laws).

**PMO** — Program Management Office. The team that runs a program. The FedRAMP PMO at GSA runs FedRAMP; many other federal programs have PMOs too.

**POA&M** — Plan of Action and Milestones. A document tracking known security weaknesses, planned remediation, owners, and target completion dates. Required at the Authorize and Monitor steps of the RMF. *See: [NIST RMF](/sam/foundation/nist-rmf).*

---

## Q

**QSA** — Qualified Security Assessor. A PCI-DSS-certified assessor authorized to perform on-site PCI compliance audits.

---

## R

**RMF** — Risk Management Framework. The 7-step lifecycle for authorizing and continuously monitoring federal information systems (Prepare / Categorize / Select / Implement / Assess / Authorize / Monitor). Defined in NIST SP 800-37. *See: [NIST RMF](/sam/foundation/nist-rmf).*

---

## S

**SAP** — Special Access Program. A category of classified work with additional access restrictions beyond the standard classified levels. Governed by JSIG.

**SAR** — Security Assessment Report. The output of a security assessment by a 3PAO or independent assessor.

**SCAP** — Security Content Automation Protocol. NIST's specification for automated security configuration assessment. STIGs are published in SCAP-compatible format.

**SCC** — SCAP Compliance Checker. DISA's free SCAP scanner.

**SOC 2** — Service Organization Control 2. AICPA's attestation framework for service providers, covering the Trust Services Criteria. Type I (point-in-time) and Type II (period over time). The de facto entry ticket for selling SaaS to enterprise customers.

**SP** — Special Publication. NIST's document type for the SP 800 series (security and privacy). Examples: SP 800-37 (RMF), SP 800-53 (controls catalog), SP 800-171 (CUI for non-federal systems), SP 800-207 (Zero Trust Architecture), SP 800-82 (OT security).

**SRG** — Security Requirements Guide. DISA's product-class-level overlay between NIST control language and product-specific STIGs.

**SSP** — System Security Plan. The master document describing a system and how it satisfies each applicable security control. Grows through the Categorize / Select / Implement steps of the RMF.

**STIG** — Security Technical Implementation Guide. DISA's per-product hardening checklists. Mandatory for DoD; widely adopted across federal civilian agencies. *See: [STIGs and CIS](/sam/foundation/stigs-and-cis).*

---

## T

**3PAO** — Third Party Assessment Organization. FedRAMP-accredited assessor that conducts security assessments for cloud services pursuing FedRAMP authorization.

---

## V

**VCDPA** — Virginia Consumer Data Protection Act. State-level privacy law.

---

## X

**XCCDF** — eXtensible Configuration Checklist Description Format. The XML schema NIST and DISA use for SCAP-compatible security checklists, including STIGs.

---

## Z

**ZTA** — Zero Trust Architecture. NIST SP 800-207's reference model. Federal agencies are required to align with it via OMB Memorandum M-22-09.
