The overlay concept (start with a generic baseline, tailor for your specific environment and risk tolerance) is what mature security programs converge on whether they're federal or not. Federal agencies overlay NIST 800-53. Healthcare orgs overlay HIPAA + NIST via HITRUST. Payment processors overlay generic security via PCI-DSS. Service providers tailor SOC 2 trust services criteria. None of the federal agency overlays apply to you, but the pattern shows up everywhere.

Doesn't apply to you in any direct sense. But the concept (start with a generic framework, tailor it for your specific environment and risk tolerance) is what mature private-sector security programs do too.

Industry-specific standards are the closest analog. PCI-DSS is essentially a "payment industry overlay" on top of generic security practice. HITRUST is a healthcare overlay. Even SOC 2 trust services criteria get tailored per service organization.

You don't need to know any specific federal agency overlay. Just know that "tailoring a framework for your context" is the pattern most large security programs converge on.
