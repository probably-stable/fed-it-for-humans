A-130 is OMB policy for federal executive-branch agencies. You're not one. The Circular is irrelevant to your day-to-day. But it's part of the federal-policy DNA that, over 25 years, leaked into private-sector practice: the CIO role as a formal executive position, privacy and security treated as one combined function, "information lifecycle management" as a discipline. You'll never need to read A-130, but the soup it's part of is the soup your industry's vocabulary swims in.

A-130 is irrelevant to you operationally. You're not a federal agency. But it's part of the larger federal-policy DNA that shaped how private-sector orgs think about information governance over the last 25 years.

The CIO role as a formal executive position. Privacy and security treated as one combined function. "Information lifecycle management" as a discipline. None of these are A-130's doing alone (the Clinger-Cohen Act, GDPR, GLBA, and many others contributed), but A-130 is one of the documents where this thinking got codified at the federal level, and federal-codified vocabulary tends to leak into private-sector practice.

You'll probably never need to read A-130. Just know it's part of the soup.
</YouPanel>
