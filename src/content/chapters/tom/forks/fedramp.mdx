You're never going to author a FedRAMP package or work for a 3PAO. But knowing what FedRAMP is changes how you read vendor pitches. When a SaaS says "FedRAMP-authorized" or "FedRAMP-ready," it's not just a sales tag — it means the service has been through (or is going through) a substantive independent assessment against a real control set. Useful signal, even if you'll never be regulated by it yourself.

<YouPanel persona="tom">
FedRAMP doesn't apply to you. But it's a useful quality signal when evaluating cloud vendors: a service that's FedRAMP-authorized has been through a rigorous independent assessment against a substantive control set, even if you're not federally regulated. The federal stamp doesn't mean the service is right for you, but it does mean someone has done the kind of security review you'd otherwise have to perform yourself.

The FedRAMP Marketplace at marketplace.fedramp.gov is publicly searchable. If you're evaluating a SaaS and want to know whether it carries any federal authorization, that's the place to check.
</YouPanel>
