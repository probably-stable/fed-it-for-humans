For you, this map mostly answers what you DON'T need to worry about. The federal-direct, defense-contractor, classified, and FedRAMP forks don't reach you. The only fork you operate on is **industry standards**, and within that, the specific frameworks depend on your industry (PCI, HIPAA, SOC 2, GDPR, state privacy laws, voluntary frameworks like NIST CSF or CIS Controls).

<YouPanel persona="tom">
You're on the **industry standards** fork only. No federal forks apply to you.

Within industry standards, the question is which industry-specific frameworks your work touches: PCI if you handle payment cards, HIPAA if you handle health data, SOC 2 if you provide services to others, ISO 27001 if you've adopted it formally, GDPR or state privacy laws if you handle EU or California resident data.

You may voluntarily adopt federal frameworks (NIST CSF is the common one) as a structure for your industry-standards work. That's a choice, not an obligation.
</YouPanel>
