<YouPanel persona="tom">
This is your home. None of the federal forks apply to you; industry standards is the entire compliance world you operate in or alongside.

Whether you're the person making framework decisions for your org or just an IT person who encounters these names in audit reports, vendor questionnaires, leadership emails, security training, or a customer's procurement form, here's the shape of each one:

- **PCI-DSS.** Applies if anyone in your org handles payment card data. Mandatory through card-brand contracts.
- **HIPAA.** U.S. federal law; applies if anyone handles health data as a covered entity or business associate.
- **SOC 2 Type II.** The de facto attestation report required to sell SaaS to enterprise customers. If your company has one, it'll come up in sales cycles and customer due diligence.
- **GDPR.** EU privacy law; applies to anyone processing EU residents' data.
- **State privacy laws.** CCPA / CPRA, VCDPA, CPA, and others as they pass. State-level analogs to GDPR.
- **CIS Controls.** Voluntary best-practices framework, often the cheapest defensible starting point for a security program.
- **NIST CSF.** Voluntary federal-authored framework; the most common bridge between federal and non-federal security thinking.

If you ARE the decider for your org's framework adoption, the pragmatic path for most small-to-mid orgs: CIS Controls IG1 as your program backbone, plus whatever industry-specific framework your sector requires, plus SOC 2 if your customers ask for it. Add ISO 27001 when you start selling internationally.

**First-week move:** make a list of every framework anyone (regulator, customer, insurer, your board, a colleague's email, a vendor questionnaire) has mentioned in the last six months. That list tells you what your org cares about, even if you're not the one deciding what to do about it.
</YouPanel>
