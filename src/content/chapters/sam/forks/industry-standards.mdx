For most people in tech, this is the world. SOC 2 reports. ISO 27001 certification. PCI-DSS audits. HIPAA. NIST CSF. GDPR and the state privacy laws. Most people in tech have at least heard of these — through work, through studying for a security certification, through a degree program, through cybersecurity media, through a friend in the field, through breach coverage in the news. They're the recognizable names, even if you've never directly worked under any of them.

What's less obvious — and what makes this chapter worth reading even if the names are familiar — is that most of these frameworks trace back to federal thinking. NIST CSF *is* federal (NIST is U.S. Department of Commerce). SOC 2's control concepts overlap heavily with NIST 800-53. ISO 27001 cross-references NIST work. HIPAA is federal law. Most of the industry standards were shaped by federal-side groundwork that came first; the industry-side branding came after.

For most people, NIST is the closest they ever get to the federal compliance regime, and they encounter it through CSF rather than 800-53 / RMF / ATO. The deeper federal mechanics covered in the foundation chapters are an edge case from the typical tech worker's perspective — even though, structurally, the foundation is what these industry standards were built on.

This chapter walks the industry-standard frameworks and notes where each connects back to federal origin.

You probably don't work in industry standards directly. But the frameworks here are referenced in the federal world more than you might expect.

NIST CSF gets cross-walked to 800-53 in many agency documentation efforts. CIS Benchmarks (which we covered earlier) come from the same org as CIS Controls and feed into federal hardening practice. SOC 2 and ISO 27001 show up in federal procurement evaluations of vendors. The federal world doesn't *require* these frameworks but does reference them.

If you ever leave fed work for industry, this is the world you'd be entering — your NIST 800-53 fluency translates more cleanly to NIST CSF and SOC 2 than to anything else in industry security.

**First-week move:** none specifically, but if your agency procures from private vendors, ask whether SOC 2 or ISO 27001 reports get reviewed as part of the procurement. The answer tells you whether industry-standard artifacts are part of your shop's vendor evaluation process.
</YouPanel>
