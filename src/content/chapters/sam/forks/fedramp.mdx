You probably don't author FedRAMP packages, but you may consume FedRAMP-authorized services as part of your fed-direct work. When your shop procures a SaaS, the question "is it FedRAMP-authorized?" comes up, and the answer often determines whether it's a viable option.

The piece worth understanding: FedRAMP authorization at one level (say Moderate) doesn't automatically satisfy your agency's needs. Your agency layers its own overlay on top: additional controls, specific configuration requirements, integration constraints. FedRAMP gets you to the starting line; your agency overlay determines whether the service crosses your specific finish line.

**First-week move:** ask whether your agency maintains a list of FedRAMP-authorized services pre-approved for use. Many do. If yes, that list is your shopping cart for SaaS procurement.
