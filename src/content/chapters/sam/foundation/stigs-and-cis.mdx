STIGs and CIS Benchmarks are where the abstract chain becomes specific machine settings. NIST 800-53 says "manage accounts." The agency overlay says "per agency policy." STIGs and CIS Benchmarks say "set `MaxAuthTries 4` in `/etc/ssh/sshd_config`." This is the implementation layer.

<YouPanel persona="sam">
This is your daily home if you're doing system hardening work. STIGs for the Linux / Windows / network gear most fed shops run, agency-specific implementation guides where your agency maintains them, vendor guides when the STIG doesn't fit cleanly, occasionally CIS for cloud workloads.

The day-to-day shape: a scan runs, findings come back, you triage by severity (CAT I first), you remediate or you write a Not Applicable / Not a Finding / exemption justification with documentation. Repeat.

**First-week move:** ask which scanner your shop uses, which baseline it checks against, AND whether your agency has its own implementation guide. The names you'll hear are some combination of SCAP Compliance Checker, OpenSCAP, CIS-CAT, Tenable Nessus / SC, or vendor-specific. Whatever the answer, that scanner's output and that agency guide together define the work in front of you.
</YouPanel>
