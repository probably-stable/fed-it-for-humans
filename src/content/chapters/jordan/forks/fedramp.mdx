FedRAMP is how cloud services get authorized for federal-agency use. Your hospital's HIPAA work doesn't depend on it: for pure HIPAA, HITRUST-certified or HIPAA-aligned cloud services are usually the more relevant reference point. But two indirect touches show up in healthcare often enough to know the program. Your federal data flows (Medicare admin, federally funded research, VA telehealth) may need to ride on FedRAMP-authorized services to satisfy their federal-side scope, and "GovCloud" offerings (AWS GovCloud, Azure Government, GCP Government Cloud) are all FedRAMP High-authorized environments that hospitals sometimes adopt for federal-grade isolation.

FedRAMP doesn't apply to your hospital's HIPAA work directly. But two indirect touches are worth knowing.

If your hospital handles federal data flows (Medicare administration, federally funded research, VA telehealth), the cloud services serving those specific systems may need FedRAMP authorization for the federal-side compliance to work.

If your hospital is moving to "GovCloud" or any federal-grade cloud environment, FedRAMP is the assurance program behind those offerings. AWS GovCloud, Azure Government, GCP Government Cloud are all FedRAMP High-authorized environments.

For pure HIPAA work, HITRUST-certified or HIPAA-aligned cloud services are usually the more relevant reference point.

**First-week move:** ask whether any of your hospital's systems use cloud services that carry FedRAMP authorization. The answer tells you whether FedRAMP is part of your compliance picture or just background context.
