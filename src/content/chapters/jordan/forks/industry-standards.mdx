This is where healthcare actually lives. HIPAA is your primary regime, NIST CSF is the most common voluntary adjunct, HITRUST is the structured certification path for orgs that want external attestation. Most of what's covered below is shared across industries, payment, SaaS, manufacturing, healthcare, but knowing the broader map is useful because your vendors, your auditors, your insurers, and your peers in other sectors all speak some piece of it.

<YouPanel persona="jordan">
You live in industry standards by default. HIPAA is your primary regime; NIST CSF is the most common voluntary adjunct; HITRUST is the certification path for orgs that want a structured external attestation.

The HIPAA Security Rule's three safeguards categories (Administrative, Physical, Technical) map roughly onto common control families across industry frameworks. The HIPAA Privacy Rule covers a different surface (use and disclosure of PHI rather than the technical security of PHI).

If your hospital is mature enough to pursue HITRUST certification, that becomes the master framework, HITRUST's CSF references HIPAA, NIST, ISO, and others, so a HITRUST-certified org can demonstrate alignment with multiple frameworks via one assessment.

**First-week move:** ask whether your hospital is HITRUST-certified, pursuing it, or running pure HIPAA-only compliance. The answer tells you the structure of your security program, and what evidence the next audit will actually look at.
</YouPanel>
