The foundation chapters walked the federal-wide chain. That spine is the same for everyone in federal IT, but the chain forks at the bottom. Different work environments add different specialized tracks: defense contractor (CMMC + 800-171), classified, cloud (FedRAMP), industry standards. Healthcare lives mainly on the industry-standards fork, but parts of a hospital can touch other forks. Knowing which fork (or forks) you're on tells you which of the next chapters you actually need.

<YouPanel persona="jordan">
You're on the **industry standards** fork by default, HIPAA is your primary regime, with NIST CSF as a common voluntary adjunct.

You may touch the **federal direct** fork on specific systems if your hospital handles federal data flows (Medicare, federally funded research, VA partnerships). You may touch **FedRAMP** indirectly if you use cloud services that have FedRAMP authorizations, though for healthcare-only data, HIPAA-aligned alternatives like HITRUST-certified services are often the pragmatic path.

**First-week move:** ask whether any of your hospital's systems carry federal-side compliance obligations on top of HIPAA. The answer tells you whether you have one fork to manage or several.
</YouPanel>
