RMF is the federal-government lifecycle that takes a system from "we want to build this" to "this system is authorized to operate" to "we're monitoring it forever." Federal agencies live inside it. Your hospital doesn't, but the shape is recognizable. The HIPAA Security Rule's risk-analysis, policy, implementation, ongoing review cycle is the same muscle in different vocabulary, and any of your systems that touch federal data flows likely runs RMF-shaped work for that specific scope.

<YouPanel persona="jordan">
Healthcare doesn't run RMF formally. But the shape is recognizable.

The HIPAA Security Rule has similar phases: risk analysis, policy and procedure development, implementation, ongoing review. The vocabulary differs (HIPAA talks about "addressable" and "required" implementation specifications instead of "controls"), but the muscle is the same: identify what needs protecting, decide what protects it, implement, verify, monitor.

If any of your hospital's systems touch federal data flows (Medicare claims, federally funded research, VA telehealth), that *specific* system likely runs RMF-shaped work for its federal-facing scope.

**First-week move:** ask whether your hospital has a documented information security risk-management process. If yes, ask what framework it follows. The answer tells you whether you're in HIPAA-only territory or HIPAA + NIST-aligned territory.
</YouPanel>
