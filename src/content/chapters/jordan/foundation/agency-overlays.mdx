Federal agencies don't implement NIST controls directly, they implement their agency's *interpretation* of NIST controls, layered as an overlay on top. The overlay is where most of the variance in fed IT compliance work lives. Your hospital doesn't have a formal agency overlay, but the same pattern shows up: HITRUST CSF and similar frameworks essentially layer healthcare-tailored interpretation on top of HIPAA + NIST + ISO.

<YouPanel persona="jordan">
No formal overlay in the federal sense applies to you. But the *shape*, start with a baseline framework, tailor for your specific environment, shows up in healthcare too.

HITRUST CSF is the most prominent healthcare-side analog: it's a tailored framework that pulls from NIST + HIPAA + ISO + others, then customizes for healthcare. Some hospitals adopt it as their internal "overlay." Smaller orgs may operate against HHS's general HIPAA guidance directly without a formal tailored framework.

**First-week move:** ask whether your hospital uses HITRUST or a similar tailored framework on top of HIPAA. The answer tells you whether you're working from a structured catalog or building documentation from scratch.
</YouPanel>
