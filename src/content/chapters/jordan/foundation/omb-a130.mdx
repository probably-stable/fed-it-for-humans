A-130 is the second federal-side link in the chain. It's OMB (Office of Management and Budget) policy that tells federal agencies how to operationalize FISMA's high-level requirements. Your hospital isn't a federal agency, so A-130 doesn't bind you. What does matter is the shape: privacy and security treated as one combined responsibility, formal accountable officers, lifecycle management of information. That architectural pattern is exactly what HIPAA Privacy Rule + HIPAA Security Rule together cover for healthcare.

<YouPanel persona="jordan">
A-130 doesn't apply to you. It governs federal executive-branch agencies, and your hospital isn't one. But the architectural shape (security and privacy treated as one combined responsibility, risk-based decision making, formal designation of accountable officers, lifecycle management of information) has analogs in healthcare.

HIPAA Privacy Rule and HIPAA Security Rule are conceptually close to A-130 Appendix II and Appendix I respectively. The vocabulary differs and the legal source differs, but the muscle is similar. If you understand A-130's shape, HIPAA's shape rhymes.

**First-week move:** if you're newly responsible for a HIPAA program, look at HHS's HIPAA Security Risk Assessment Tool. It's the closest healthcare-side analog to the structured risk-based framework A-130 demands of federal agencies, and it'll teach you the shape without the federal jargon.
</YouPanel>
