This is where abstract control language meets specific machine settings. NIST 800-53 says "manage accounts." STIGs and CIS Benchmarks say "set `MaxAuthTries 4` in `/etc/ssh/sshd_config`." STIGs are DISA's federal-side checklists (mandatory for DoD); CIS Benchmarks are voluntary and widely adopted across healthcare for general server hardening. For your medical-device fleet specifically, the FDA's pre-market guidance and NIST SP 800-82 (OT security) tend to matter more than either.

Healthcare doesn't formally use STIGs unless you're touching federal data flows. CIS Benchmarks are more relevant, voluntary, designed for general IT, widely adopted in healthcare for general server hardening.

For medical devices specifically, the FDA's pre-market guidance for medical-device cybersecurity is the more relevant document, and it references SP 800-82 (the OT security guide) more than STIGs.

**First-week move:** ask whether your hospital does formal configuration hardening against CIS Benchmarks or another framework, and how the results get reported. The answer tells you whether you have a structured hardening program or an ad-hoc one.
