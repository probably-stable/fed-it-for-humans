The overlay layer is where most of the variance in fed IT compliance lives. For you, this matters in two stacked ways: FedRAMP itself is a cloud-tailored overlay on top of NIST 800-53, and your customer agencies then layer THEIR overlays on top of FedRAMP. A federal agency using your service is satisfying a stack, NIST 800-53 → FedRAMP overlay (where you live) → their agency overlay (where they live). The customer-overlay layer is where their additional asks come from.

FedRAMP is essentially a "cloud overlay" on NIST 800-53, it tailors the federal baseline specifically for cloud services. Your federal customer agencies then layer THEIR own agency overlays on top of FedRAMP.

So a federal agency using your service is satisfying a stack: NIST 800-53 → FedRAMP overlay (your service is authorized against this) → their agency overlay (their internal controls layer on top). You provide visibility and evidence at the FedRAMP layer; your customer handles their agency-specific layer themselves.

**First-week move:** ask which agency overlays your largest customer agencies apply on top of FedRAMP. Some agencies have minimal additional overlay; others have substantial extra requirements that flow through to you via contract clauses or via the customer's continuous monitoring asks.
</YouPanel>
