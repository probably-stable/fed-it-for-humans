A-130 is OMB policy that tells federal agencies how to operationalize FISMA. You're not a federal agency, so it doesn't bind you directly. But the agency CIO making your ATO decision is operating under A-130, the annual reauthorization cadence, the continuous monitoring expectation, the documentation requirements you face all trace back to it. Your FedRAMP package is essentially the artifact that lets a federal customer say "yes, you satisfy what A-130 requires of any service we use."

<YouPanel persona="gary">
A-130 is what your federal agency customers are following when they decide whether to authorize your service. The agency CIO making your ATO decision is operating under A-130. The annual reauthorization cadence, the continuous monitoring expectation, the documentation requirements, all A-130-driven.

Your FedRAMP package is essentially the artifact that lets a federal customer say "yes, you satisfy what A-130 requires of any service we use." You're never directly held to A-130; your customer is, and they pass the requirement through to you via FedRAMP.

**First-week move:** ask whoever owns your FedRAMP program which agency authorizations you currently hold. The list tells you who your real "customers" are for compliance purposes, those agencies are the ones referencing A-130 when they reauthorize you each year.
</YouPanel>
