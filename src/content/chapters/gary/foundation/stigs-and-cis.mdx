<YouPanel persona="gary">
For your own infrastructure (the stuff you control), CIS Benchmarks are usually the practical default. CIS Hardened Images for cloud VMs simplify the baseline-establishment problem dramatically.

For the FedRAMP-facing parts of your service, you may need to demonstrate STIG or CIS alignment depending on what your customer agencies expect. Your 3PAO will tell you which.

Cloud provider security best practices (AWS Well-Architected, Azure Security Benchmark, GCP security best practices) are essentially vendor guides at the cloud layer. They often cover controls that STIGs/CIS don't, because they're cloud-architecture-specific (IAM, network segmentation, encryption-at-rest with provider key management, etc.).

**First-week move:** ask which baseline your operations team scans your VMs against. CIS, STIG, vendor-specific, or a custom in-house baseline are all common answers; each tells you something about how your security org thinks.
