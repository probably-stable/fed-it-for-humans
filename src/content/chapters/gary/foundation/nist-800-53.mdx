<YouPanel persona="gary">
800-53 IS your job. FedRAMP authorization packages are essentially documentation of how your service satisfies a tailored 800-53 baseline. Your SSP has a section per applicable control. Your continuous monitoring tracks evidence per control. Your 3PAO assesses per control.

You'll spend more time inside this document than any other NIST publication combined. Get fluent in your baseline (Low, Moderate, High, or Li-SaaS) and the enhancements that apply.

FedRAMP also has inheritance — your service likely inherits a chunk of controls from your underlying cloud provider (AWS, Azure, GCP all have FedRAMP-authorized infrastructure with documented inheritable controls). Knowing which controls you inherit from your IaaS / PaaS provider vs. which you implement yourself is foundational to your SSP.

**First-week move:** ask for the FedRAMP baseline document for your authorization level, and ask which controls your service inherits from your underlying cloud provider. Together those two answers define the actual surface area you own.
</YouPanel>

## What's next

The next chapter covers **agency overlays** — how individual federal agencies tailor the NIST baseline. For you, this matters because your federal customer agencies layer their own overlays on top of FedRAMP. Knowing which overlays apply to which customers helps you anticipate the additional asks that come through during agency authorization or continuous monitoring.
