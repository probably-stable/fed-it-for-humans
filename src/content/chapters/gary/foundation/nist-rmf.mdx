If you're running a FedRAMP program, you're running the RMF, just in cloud-services dress. The seven steps are the same. The cast changes: a 3PAO instead of an agency assessor, an Agency AO or the JAB instead of a single agency AO, FedRAMP's continuous monitoring program instead of an agency-specific ConMon shop. Knowing the underlying RMF shape makes the FedRAMP-specific variations easier to anticipate.

FedRAMP is essentially the RMF tailored for cloud services. You go through the same seven steps in FedRAMP flavor: Prepare, Categorize, Select (from a FedRAMP baseline), Implement, Assess (by a 3PAO instead of an agency assessor), Authorize (by an Agency AO or by the JAB), Monitor (FedRAMP's continuous monitoring program).

The most distinctive thing about the cloud-services version: the **3PAO** (Third Party Assessment Organization). Federal agencies can't use just any assessor for FedRAMP; the assessor has to be FedRAMP-accredited. That's the big procedural difference from a direct federal ATO.

**First-week move:** ask who runs your assessments. Then look that 3PAO up on the FedRAMP marketplace. The 3PAO's reputation and depth of experience with your tech stack matters a lot to how smooth your assessments go.
