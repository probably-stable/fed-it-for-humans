<YouPanel persona="gary">
This IS your job. FedRAMP is the federal-side authorization that lets your service sell to federal agencies, and most of your compliance program revolves around it.

Things you'll spend time on:

- **The SSP** — the master document describing how your service satisfies the FedRAMP baseline. Hundreds of pages, alive document, versioned.
- **3PAO engagement** — the assessment cycle. SAP (Security Assessment Plan), assessment fieldwork, SAR (Security Assessment Report).
- **POA&M maintenance** — monthly updates on findings + remediation timelines. The federal customer agencies watch this.
- **Continuous monitoring** — vulnerability scans, change management, ongoing evidence collection.
- **Customer agency communication** — agencies that leverage your authorization may have their own questions, additional overlay requirements, customer-specific reporting expectations.

The skill that separates good FedRAMP teams from struggling ones: knowing how to interpret a control in your specific cloud architecture without re-deriving the answer every time. Build internal control narratives that you can reuse and update; don't write new prose for every assessment cycle.

**First-week move:** ask which path your service is on (Agency or JAB), which sponsoring agency authorized you (if Agency path), what impact level your current authorization is at, and which 3PAO does your assessments. Those four facts define the scope of your federal-side compliance work.
</YouPanel>

## What's next

The next chapter covers the **industry standards** fork — where the non-federal-facing parts of your service live. SOC 2 Type II as the enterprise-customer entry ticket, ISO 27001 if you sell internationally, plus whatever industry-specific frameworks your customers care about.
