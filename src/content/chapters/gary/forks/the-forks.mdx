The foundation chapters walked the federal-wide chain. That spine is the same for everyone in federal IT — but the chain forks at the bottom. For you, your main federal-side surface is **FedRAMP**; your customers' agency-overlay layer is the federal-direct fork as it reaches you; your internal infrastructure (the stuff customers don't see) lives on **industry standards**. If you serve DoD customers with CUI workloads, the **defense-contractor** fork (CMMC) may also flow through to you via their requirements.

<YouPanel persona="gary">
You're on the **FedRAMP** fork as your service's main federal-side surface. You may also touch the **federal direct** fork via the agency-overlay layer your customers apply on top of FedRAMP.

If you have DoD customers with CUI workloads in their own environments, the **defense contractor** fork (CMMC) becomes part of THEIR compliance picture and may flow through to you via contract terms — but the deep treatment of CMMC itself is out of scope for this guide.

For your own internal infrastructure (the stuff your customers don't see), you're on the **industry standards** fork — SOC 2 / ISO 27001 / CIS / your industry's specific frameworks.

**First-week move:** ask which forks your service is currently operating against. Most cloud services touch at least two (FedRAMP for federal-side, industry standards for internal). Knowing which is which helps you sort the work.
</YouPanel>
