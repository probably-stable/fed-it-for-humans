For your service's federal-side surface, FedRAMP is the conversation. For everything else — your internal infrastructure, your relationships with non-federal customers, your insurance posture, your enterprise sales motion — you're in industry-standards territory.

The most common stack for a federal-selling SaaS: SOC 2 Type II as the enterprise-customer entry ticket, ISO 27001 if you sell internationally, CIS Controls as the underlying program structure, NIST CSF for cyber insurance and broader credibility, plus whatever industry-specific frameworks your customers care about (HIPAA for health, PCI for payments, etc.).

Many of these align with FedRAMP underneath. The work to satisfy SOC 2 substantially overlaps with the work for FedRAMP Moderate. Smart compliance teams design once and harvest the artifacts across multiple frameworks.

**First-week move:** ask which industry-standard frameworks your company carries (SOC 2, ISO 27001, CSF alignment, etc.) and which are pursued vs. just claimed. The answer tells you what the actual evidence shape is — attestation reports, certification certificates, alignment statements — and where each lives.
</YouPanel>
